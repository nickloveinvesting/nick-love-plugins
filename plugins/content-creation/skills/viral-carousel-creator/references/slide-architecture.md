## The 10-Slide Architecture

A viral carousel is a narrative sequence engineered for completion. Every slide has a specific job.

| Slide | Function | Execution |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **1** | THE HOOK (Gatekeeper) | Stop the scroll. One idea. Under 10 words. High contrast. Targets System 1 brain. Carries 80% of the weight. |
| **2** | TENSION + AGITATION | Do NOT provide solution yet. Validate the hook, amplify the pain, introduce surprise or frustration. Transitions user from curiosity to emotional investment. |
| **3-5** | CORE VALUE (Progressive Disclosure) | Each slide = one main point. Flashcard, not paragraph. Each swipe delivers micro-dopamine hit. Satisfies System 2 processing. |
| **6-7** | BUILD + PROOF | Stack trust. Data point, case study, or "this is what happened when" slide. Removes logical objections. Make saves rational. |
| **8** | OPEN LOOP | Tease what is coming. "But here's what most people miss..." pulls them forward. |
| **9** | PAYOFF | Deliver the promised insight, transformation moment, or surprising conclusion. |
| **10** | CTA (Terminal) | Single, specific, unambiguous action. Do NOT offer multiple choices (triggers choice overload). "Save this" OR "Comment SYSTEM" — not both. |

### The Psychological Function of Each Phase

**Slide 1 — The Gatekeeper:** Its exclusive function is to disrupt scrolling, trigger System 1, and create a severe curiosity gap. Minimal text, high contrast, direct address to target demographic. If the gatekeeper fails, the rest of the carousel is irrelevant.

**Slide 2 — Tension Builder:** The user expects immediate gratification after swiping. Withholding it builds tension. This slide validates the hook, agitates the pain, and introduces a shocking stat, expert contradiction, or visceral amplification. It establishes the WHY — why the user must keep swiping.

**Slides 3-6/8 — Progressive Disclosure:** Information delivered sequentially, one point per slide, to prevent choice overload. Each slide focuses on exactly one step, rule, or insight. Cramming multiple steps onto one slide destroys pacing and spikes cognitive load.

**Penultimate Slide — Social Proof:** Before the CTA, remove lingering objections. Quantifiable success metrics, testimonials, brief case study, or social consensus. Primes the user to accept the final instruction.

**Final Slide — Terminal CTA:** Must dictate a single action. Multiple choices ("Like, comment, share, save, AND click link in bio!") trigger choice overload, causing NO action. Match the CTA to content type: educational framework → "Save this." Debate content → "Which step do you disagree with? Comments."

### Slide Pacing Rules

- **One point per slide.** Think flashcard, not blog paragraph.
- **Keep visual rhythm consistent.** Same layout structure, change the accent element to avoid monotony.
- **Use open loops between slides.** Tease what is coming ("Next: the fix that takes 2 minutes").
- **Make saves rational:** templates, scripts, checklists, decision trees, frameworks.
- **Make shares social:** "Send this to the person who handles hiring."
- **Put the CTA twice:** one soft CTA mid-way (slide 5-6), one clear CTA on the last slide.
- **Write for skimmers:** bold the key phrase; keep supporting text short.
- **Alternate layouts** to prevent monotony: statement slide → list slide → proof slide → statement slide.

### Slide Count Dynamics

Two optimal lengths depending on content complexity and goal:

**Rapid-Fire Sequence (3-5 Slides):**
- Best for: simple concepts, myth-busting, quick lists, product showcases
- Advantage: lowest cognitive friction, highest Completion Rate
- Limitation: limited space for deep storytelling or objection handling
- Strongest for: initial algorithmic reach signal

**Deep-Dive Sequence (7-10 Slides):**
- Best for: educational guides, step-by-step cascades, complex narratives
- Advantage: higher CTR, CVR, and Save ratios
- Allows: thorough problem agitation, progressive value delivery, trust building before CTA
- Gold standard for conversion-focused content

| Slide Count | Best For | Completion Rate | Conversion Strength |
|-------------|----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 3-5 slides | Quick tips, single concept, viral reach | Highest completion | Moderate |
| 6-8 slides | Frameworks, listicles, tutorials | Strong balance | Strong |
| 9-10 slides | Story arcs, deep dives, case studies | Requires tight arc | Highest (with PAS/AIDA) |
| 10+ slides | Only if every slide delivers clear value | Drops after slide 8 | Risky without strong pacing |

### AIDA Adapted for Carousels

| Slide Range | AIDA Stage | Purpose |
|------------|------------|---------|
| Slide 1 | Attention | Stop scroll with bold claim or pain point |
| Slides 2-4 | Interest | Name the problem, add context, share data |
| Slides 5-8 | Desire/Detail | Provide framework, show proof, case study |
| Slides 9-10 | Action | Single clear action, curiosity-driven tease |

## Content Frameworks

### Framework 1: Hook-Problem-Value-CTA (HPVC)

The most widely used carousel structure. Works for educational, promotional, and authority content.

1. **Slide 1 — Hook:** Bold, curiosity-driven or benefit-driven statement
2. **Slide 2 — Problem/Pain Confirmation:** Names the frustration, objection, or stakes
3. **Slides 3-6 — Value Delivery:** 3-5 concise tips, steps, or insights that solve the problem
4. **Final Slide — CTA:** Save, share, comment, follow, or join lead magnet

### Framework 2: Triple-Hook

Optimized for Instagram's carousel re-serving behavior. Instagram sometimes re-shows carousels starting from a later slide.

- Slides 1-3 each function as standalone hooks (three different angles, all entry points)
- Slides 4+ deliver the promised value
- Increases probability that at least one hook resonates with each viewer
- Even if viewers drop off, the strongest impact has already occurred

### Framework 3: Micro-Hook Ladder

Every slide contains a phrase, question, or cliffhanger that keeps users swiping.

Techniques:
- Ending slides with incomplete statements ("The real mistake? On the next slide.")
- Numbered lists where viewers feel compelled to see all items
- Repeated pattern lines ("Next, do this…") that build rhythm and expectation
- Progressive revelation where each slide adds one piece to a larger picture

### Framework 4: Story Arc (Before-Mess-After)

Narrative structure that maps well to coaching, consulting, and transformation brands.

1. **Before:** Show the initial situation or pain
2. **Mess:** Highlight the breaking point, failed attempts, or key realization
3. **After:** Reveal the new system, strategy, or mindset with specific outcomes
4. **CTA:** Invite users to learn the full system

### Framework 5: Step-by-Step Tutorial

Highest save rate format. Viewers save to revisit steps later.

- Dedicate one clear action per slide
- Use numbers prominently ("Step 1," "Step 2")
- Each slide must stand alone as a screenshot-able mini-instruction
- The full sequence tells a complete implementation story

### Framework 6: Problem Cascade

Shows escalating consequences of a common mistake or broken system.

- Slide 1: Name the initial mistake
- Slides 2-7: Each slide shows the next consequence that follows
- Slide 8-9: The breaking point and the alternative path
- Slide 10: CTA

This format drives high share rates because viewers tag people who need to see it.

## CTA Tactics

### High-Performing CTA Formats

- **Save CTA:** "Save this so you don't forget these steps" — targets the strongest quality signal in 2026
- **Comment keyword CTA:** "Comment 'SYSTEM' and I'll DM you the checklist" — generates thousands of comments and algorithm signals
- **Share CTA:** "Send this to the person who handles hiring" — names a specific recipient, increasing share probability
- **Follow CTA:** "Follow for more frameworks like this" — simple, low-friction
- **DM CTA:** "DM 'EMPIRE' for the free diagnostic" — direct lead gen
- **Link CTA:** "Tap the link in bio to get the full template" — conversion-focused

### CTA Placement Rules

1. **Soft CTA at slide 5-6:** A save or share prompt mid-carousel captures intent before potential drop-off
2. **Hard CTA on final slide:** Explicit instruction with visual emphasis (accent color, large text, clear direction)
3. **Caption CTA:** Reinforce the final slide CTA in the post caption. Both platforms surface captions alongside the carousel.

### CTA Design Rules

- CTA slide should use reversed colors or distinct treatment from body slides (e.g., gold background with dark text if body slides are dark with light text)
- CTA text should be 30-44pt minimum — must be the most prominent element
- Include one clear action, not three. "Save this" OR "Comment SYSTEM" — not both.

## The 7-Step Hook Writing System

Repeatable process for generating high-swipe hooks in under 15 minutes.

1. **Identify the burning pain or desire.** Check comments, DMs, sales call transcripts, TikTok search bar autocomplete for exact audience language.
2. **Choose ONE specific, measurable outcome.** "Add $500K without hiring" not "grow your business."
3. **Match the emotion to the hook type.** Fear of doing it wrong = Mistake Hook. Desire for result = Promise Hook. Curiosity = Cliffhanger.
4. **Write 10 versions in under 10 minutes.** No filtering. No editing. Volume first.
5. **Cut every unnecessary word.** Read aloud. If you pause or stumble, delete and rewrite.
6. **The brutal test:** "Would I stop mid-scroll and swipe for this?" If no, rewrite.
7. **Pair with a high-contrast visual** that emotionally matches the hook.

**A/B testing:** Post two different hooks on the same carousel content one week apart. The winner typically gets 2-4x higher reach.

## Content Types That Perform

| Content Type | Why It Works | Structure |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Educational Listicles** | Each item = one slide. High save rate. | HPVC or Step-by-Step |
| **Step-by-Step Frameworks** | Mirrors instruction-following behavior. | Tutorial Framework |
| **Myth Busting** | High comment rates from debate. | HPVC with Mistake Hook |
| **Before/After Transformation** | Visual tension drives completion. | Story Arc |
| **Problem Cascade** | Shows escalating consequences. Highly shareable. | Problem Cascade |
| **Contrarian Take** | Pattern interrupt. Triggers strong reactions. | HPVC with Shock Hook |
| **Personal Story Arc** | Vulnerability + proof. | Story Arc |
| **Comparison Content** | "X vs Y" — each slide covers one point. | Micro-Hook Ladder |
| **Product/Offer Showcase** | Multiple angles, features, or use cases. | Step-by-Step or HPVC |

## Seven Fatal Carousel Mistakes

1. **Too many words on slide 1.** Over 12 words kills it. The brain cannot process a paragraph at scroll speed. Target 8-10 words.

2. **Medium contrast backgrounds.** Soft tones make text unreadable at speed. Use extreme contrast only (black on white, white on obsidian).

3. **No narrative arc.** Random slides without logical flow from problem to insight to resolution. No pull to continue swiping.

4. **Burying the value.** Starting with context or setup before delivering anything useful. Slide 2 must be instant value, not warmup.

5. **Every slide looks the same.** Visual monotony causes swipe fatigue. Alternate layouts while maintaining brand consistency.

6. **Weak or missing CTA.** Without explicit instruction on the last slide, engagement drops 30-50%. Always include a specific action.

7. **Using video slideshow instead of native Photo Mode on TikTok.** Native Photo Mode gets the algorithmic boost. Video slideshows are treated as video content and miss carousel-specific distribution.

## Campaign Cadence and Planning

### Recommended Weekly Cadence

| Day | Content Type | Hook Style | CTA Goal |
|-----|-------------|------------|----------|
| Monday | Myth Bust / Contrarian | Mistake Hook | Comment (debate) |
| Wednesday | Framework / How-To | Promise Hook | Save (reference) |
| Friday | Story / Personal Arc | Cliffhanger Hook | Share / DM keyword |

### Production Workflow

1. Script the carousel using the 10-slide architecture. Hooks first, CTA second, fill the middle.
2. Design at 1080x1920px (9:16) using brand templates.
3. Apply the Squint Test on slide 1.
4. Add trending TikTok sound that matches content mood.
5. Write caption with embedded keywords for TikTok search indexing.
6. Upload via native Photo Mode (not video slideshow).
7. Cross-post to Instagram with aspect ratio adjusted to 1080x1350 (4:5).

### One-Day Content Sprint

Create a month of carousel content in one session:

**Pre-Sprint (Night Before):**
- Review top 20 carousels in your niche from last 30 days
- List 10 questions your audience asks repeatedly
- Note 3 controversial takes you genuinely believe
- Gather 5 personal stories or results to share

**Hours 1-2:** Select frameworks and match to topics
**Hours 3-4:** Write 3 hook options for each carousel, select strongest
**Hours 5-6:** Script all slides using 10-slide architecture
**Hours 7-8:** Design in batches using templates, schedule, and plan engagement

**Output:** 10 carousels + 9 hook variations = 19 content pieces. With cross-posting: 30+ posts.
