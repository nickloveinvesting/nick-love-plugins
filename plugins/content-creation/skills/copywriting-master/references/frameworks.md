# Copywriting Frameworks Deep Dive

## PAS Framework (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

### Overview
The most versatile and powerful copywriting framework. Works for any length from tweets to sales pages.

### Structure Breakdown

**Problem (15% of copy)**
- Identify the specific, tangible problem
- Use language your audience uses
- Be specific, not generic
- Show you understand their situation

**Agitate (25% of copy)**
- Make them feel the pain
- Describe consequences of inaction
- Use emotional language
- Paint the picture of their current struggle
- This is the most overlooked and most powerful step

**Solution (60% of copy)**
- Introduce your product/service as the answer
- Show how it specifically solves the problems mentioned
- Provide proof it works
- Make the next step clear

## AIDA Framework (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

### Overview
Classic framework from 1898. Best for structured sales pages and ads.

### Structure Breakdown

**Attention (Hook)**
- Stop the scroll
- Make a bold claim
- Ask a provocative question
- State a surprising fact
- Time: First 1-5 seconds

**Interest**
- Expand on the hook
- Explain why they should care
- Introduce the topic/problem
- Build curiosity

**Desire**
- Show the transformation
- Use social proof
- Paint the after picture
- Address objections
- Build emotional connection

**Action**
- Clear CTA
- Remove friction
- Create urgency (if authentic)
- Provide guarantee

## StoryBrand Framework

### Overview
Positions the customer as the hero. Your brand is the guide. Based on Donald Miller's book.

### The 7-Part Structure

1. A Character (Hero) = Your customer
2. Has a Problem = External, Internal, Philosophical
3. Meets a Guide = Your brand (empathy + authority)
4. Who Gives Them a Plan = Clear steps to success
5. Calls Them to Action = Direct CTA
6. That Helps Them Avoid Failure = Stakes/consequences
7. And Ends in Success = Transformation achieved

## BAB Framework (Before-After-Bridge)

### Overview
Simple transformation framework. Great for shorter copy.

### Structure

**Before**
- Current painful state
- Where they are now
- The struggle they face

**After**
- Desired future state
- Where they want to be
- Life after the problem is solved

**Bridge**
- Your product/service
- How to get from Before to After
- The path forward

### BAB Example

BEFORE:
Right now, you are spending 10+ hours per week on 
social media content that gets minimal engagement.

AFTER:

## Choosing the Right Framework

### Use PAS When:
- Writing sales pages
- Email sequences
- Social media posts
- Any persuasive copy

### Use AIDA When:
- Creating structured landing pages
- Writing long-form ads
- Building sales presentations

### Use StoryBrand When:
- Defining overall brand messaging
- Creating website copy
- Building marketing campaigns

### Use BAB When:
- Writing short-form copy
- Social posts
- Email subject lines
- Quick pitches
