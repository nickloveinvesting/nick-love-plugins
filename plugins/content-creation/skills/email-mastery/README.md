## Pro Tips

**Cold Email**:
- Don't judge campaign success on Email 1 (follow-up emails generate 70% of replies)
- Always A/B test subject lines
- 50-120 word emails outperform longer copy
- Personal P.S. gets high readership—use strategically

**HTML Design**:
- Tables only—no flexbox or grid
- Padding on cells, not margin
- MSO conditionals required for Outlook
- Test across Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, mobile

**Copywriting**:
- Lead with specific numbers ("15 deals in 30 days" not "more deals")
- One goal per email
- Rhythm matters (short + medium + long sentences)
- Sounds like human conversation, not marketing speak

**Sequencing**:
- Email 1 hook is NOT your best email (it's just to get them to open Email 2)
- Vary your angles across the sequence
- Build to Email 6 (direct ask)
- Email 7 is honest breakup (shows respect)

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use this for B2B SaaS?**
A: Yes. Examples are real estate-focused, but frameworks work for any B2B.

**Q: What about transactional emails (receipts, password reset)?**
A: Not covered in detail, but core principles apply. Focus on clarity, one action, helpful tone.

**Q: How do I avoid sounding like AI?**
A: See "Gary Provost Rhythm Principles" and "Anti-Slop Guardrails" in main SKILL.md.

**Q: Can I use HTML templates as-is?**
A: Yes, but update colors/text/images to match YOUR brand. Unique is better than default.

**Q: What about segmentation?**
A: See "Segmentation & Personalization" section. Segment by lifecycle stage first.

**Q: Should I use emojis?**
A: No. They hurt deliverability and look less professional.
