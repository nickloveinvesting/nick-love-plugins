# Copywriting Frameworks Reference

## Quick Reference: Which Framework to Use

| Framework | Best For | Email Type | Complexity |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **PAS** | Pain-driven messaging | Cold email, pain-focused | Simple |
| **AIDA** | Awareness building | Promotional, launches | Medium |
| **StoryBrand** | Brand storytelling | Welcome, founder story | Complex |
| **Direct Response** | High-urgency conversion | Limited-time offers | Advanced |

---

## PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) Template

**Word Count**: 80-100 words
**Best For**: Cold email, sales sequences, objection handling

```
Hi [Name],

PROBLEM:
[Specific, quantified pain point they have]

AGITATE:
[Consequences if they don't fix this]
[Emotional + financial impact]

SOLUTION:
[How you solve it]
[Specific outcome/metric]

[CTA]

[Name]
```

**Example** (Real Estate):
```
Hi James,

PROBLEM:
Most fix-and-flip investors underestimate renovation costs by 15-25%.

AGITATE:
That $500K deal with expected $75K profit turns into $30K. An extra $45K gets eaten by unexpected costs.

SOLUTION:
Our system predicts costs within 3-5% accuracy. Clients capture $12K-$18K per deal back.

See the difference in your market?

[Schedule Call]
```

## AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action) Template

**Word Count**: 100-150 words
**Best For**: Marketing campaigns, feature announcements, value-focused messaging

```
Hi [Name],

ATTENTION:
[Bold claim or surprising statement]

INTEREST:
[Specific details that expand on claim]
[Build curiosity with data/examples]

DESIRE:
[Benefits + how it makes them feel]
[Social proof or testimonial]

ACTION:
[Clear CTA with reduced friction]

[Name]
```

**Example** (Private Lending):
```
Hi Sarah,

ATTENTION:
We found a way to make private lending returns market-proof.

INTEREST:
By focusing on fix-and-flip loans in high-growth markets with strict DSC ratios, our portfolio has averaged 16.8% annual returns for 4 years—even in downturns.

DESIRE:
Our investors sleep better. Predictable 12-month returns with real collateral backing.

"Predictable returns with predictable risk. That's everything I wanted." - James M., Accredited Investor

ACTION:
See our track record and current opportunity set.

[View Opportunities]
```

## StoryBrand (7-Point Framework) Template

**Structure**: Character → Problem → Guide → Plan → CTA → Failure → Success
**Best For**: Welcome sequences, founder stories, brand positioning

```
Hi [Name],

CUSTOMER/CHARACTER:
You've [achieved something]. But you're struggling with [new problem].

PROBLEM:
[Specific pain they feel]

GUIDE:
[Who you are - credibility]
[Why you understand their struggle]

PLAN:
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

CTA:
[Clear action to take]

FAILURE:
If you don't act, you'll be stuck in [bad state].

SUCCESS:
If you do, you'll achieve [desired outcome].

[Name]
```

## Direct Response (Sugarman-Style) Template

**For**: High-urgency campaigns, limited-time offers, conversion sequences

### Step 1: Headline
Specific outcome, quantified if possible.
```
"How one investor found 15 deals in 30 days"
"Increase deal margins 18% in 90 days"
"Build a real estate team without hiring"
```

### Step 2: Subheadline
Expand on the headline, build curiosity.

### Step 3: Problem Quantification
Do the math. Make it concrete.
```
"Marcus spent 6 hours/week prospecting = 312 hours/year
Result: 2-3 deals/quarter
Cost per deal: $300-400 in time"
```

### Step 4: "So What" Chain
Drill down to emotional consequence:
1. Feature → Functional benefit
2. Functional benefit → Financial benefit
3. Financial benefit → Emotional benefit

### Step 5: Proof
Specific case study or testimonial with numbers.

### Step 6: Disqualification
Who this isn't for (creates urgency, improves list quality).
```
This works if:
✅ You're serious about scaling
✅ You have capital to close
✅ You're willing to execute

This doesn't work if:
❌ You want a shortcut
❌ You're uncommitted
❌ [Alternative that doesn't fit]
```

### Step 7: Offer
Clear ask with friction reducers.
```
$5K. 28-day implementation. 60-day guarantee (full refund if no results).
Only 3 spots left.
```

### Step 8: Urgency
Real scarcity (not manufactured).

## Subject Line Formulas

| Pattern | Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Curiosity** | "Quick question about [their thing]" | "Quick question about your OKC expansion" |
| **Specific** | "[Number] ways to [outcome] without [pain]" | "3 ways to increase margins without cutting costs" |
| **Social Proof** | "How [Company] achieved [result]" | "How one investor found 15 deals in 30 days" |
| **Personalization** | "[Name], I noticed [signal]" | "[Name], saw you just licensed as hard money lender" |
| **Question** | "Do you [common struggle]?" | "Do you leave money on every deal?" |
| **Urgency/Scarcity** | "[Timeframe] left until [consequence]" | "Only 2 spots left before next cohort starts" |

## Openings That Hook

**Pattern 1: Story Start**
```
"Three months ago, our deal pipeline dried up. I thought we were done."
```

**Pattern 2: Bold Claim**
```
"Your contractor just quoted 40% over budget."
```

**Pattern 3: Specific Data**
```
"47 investors analyzed 1,200 deals this quarter. Here's what they learned."
```

**Pattern 4: Question**
```
"What if your deal margins could increase 18% without changing anything else?"
```

**Pattern 5: Confession**
```
"I almost quit real estate investing four times. Here's why I didn't."
```

## CTA Formulas

✅ **Strong CTAs** (action verb + specific outcome):
- "See how [Company] scaled to 8 deals/month"
- "Get placed in next IGNITE cohort"
- "View market analysis for [City]"
- "Schedule strategy call"
- "Claim your spot"

❌ **Weak CTAs** (vague):
- "Click here"
- "Learn more"
- "Submit"
- "Sign up"

## A/B Testing Ideas

**Email 1: Subject Lines**
- Curiosity vs Benefit-Focused
- Personalized vs Generic
- Question vs Statement

**Email 2: Email Length**
- Short (50 words) vs Medium (100 words)
- Measure: Reply rate

**Email 3: CTA Type**
- Meeting request ("Schedule call") vs Reply-based ("Reply with yes/no")
- Measure: Reply rate + quality

**Email 4: Proof Type**
- Stats vs Testimonial vs Case Study
- Measure: Engagement

**Email 5: Send Time**
- 9-10 AM vs 1-2 PM vs 5-6 PM
- Measure: Open rate

## Common Objection Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I'm not interested" | "No problem. Quick question—what would make this relevant?" |
| "Send me something" | "Will do. Quick question first—what's your biggest challenge right now?" |
| "I don't have time" | "Totally get it. This is just a 15-minute conversation. Would [day/time] work?" |
| "I'm good right now" | "Totally understand. Quick question—when was the last time you reviewed [topic]?" |
| "Your solution is expensive" | "Fair point. Quick question—how much are you losing annually to [problem]?" |

## Writing Checklist

**Before sending, verify:**
- [ ] Specific numbers (not adjectives)
- [ ] One goal per email (not multiple CTAs)
- [ ] Proof or data backing claim
- [ ] Personalization (shows research)
- [ ] Rhythm varied (short + long sentences)
- [ ] Sounds like human (read aloud)
- [ ] No AI language (delve, leverage, landscape)
- [ ] Mobile-friendly length
- [ ] Clear unsubscribe
- [ ] Test variables identified
