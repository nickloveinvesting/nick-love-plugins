# Cold Email Sequence Examples

## Complete 7-Email Cold Sequence: Real Estate Market Analysis

**Target Audience**: Fix-and-flip investors in expanding markets
**Goal**: Schedule discovery call
**Duration**: 14 days
**Expected Response Rate**: 8-12%

---

### EMAIL 1: The Hook (Day 0, 10 AM)

**Subject**: Quick question about your [city] expansion

**Preheader**: I found a pattern in your market

**Body**:

```
Hi [FirstName],

Saw your LinkedIn update about expanding into [market]—smart move.

I just finished analyzing 47 deals in that market and found a pattern most investors miss (it's costing them $8K-$12K per deal).

Worth a quick 15-minute conversation?

[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Company]

P.S. — The deals I'm referencing just closed last month, so data is fresh.
```

**Why this works**:
- Specific observation (proves research)
- Quantified pain ($8K-$12K cost)
- Short (79 words—easy to read)
- Clear single CTA

---

### EMAIL 2: The Value Proof (Day 1, 11 AM)

**Subject**: How [Company Name] increased margins 18% in 90 days

**Body**:

```
Hi [FirstName],

Quick follow-up on [my previous email about your market].

I wanted to share a real example since you're entering [market]. 

[Company Name] was doing 8-10 deals/quarter in a similar market. When we analyzed their last 15 deals, we found they were leaving roughly $12K-$18K on the table per deal through a missed analysis step.

Sounds small per deal, but over a year that's $144K-$216K in lost profits.

Once they implemented our standard, their margins on new deals increased 18% without changing anything else.

Think that's relevant to your expansion?

[Your Name]

P.S. — Happy to run the same analysis on your last 3 deals (free). No obligation.
```

**Why this works**:
- Specific case study with company name (credible)
- Quantified before/after metrics
- Shows value of your work
- P.S. is a micro-commitment

---

### EMAIL 3: Different Angle (Day 3, 2 PM)

**Subject**: Different thought about your [market]

**Body**:

```
Hi [FirstName],

Quick thought—deal flow might not be your biggest challenge in [market].

Most investors expanding into new markets struggle with something different: accurate cost estimation in unfamiliar territory.

The contractors, labor rates, and building codes vary significantly. Miss those and you're underwater on day one.

Curious if that's on your radar?

[Your Name]

P.S. — If deal sourcing is actually your bottleneck, happy to address that instead.
```

**Why this works**:
- Reframes around different pain point
- Non-pushy ("if that's on your radar")
- Shows understanding of market challenges
- P.S. gives them control

---

### EMAIL 4: Social Proof (Day 5, 10:30 AM)

**Subject**: [Mutual Connection] suggested I reach out

**Body**:

```
Hi [FirstName],

Was talking with [Name] at [Company] last week about expanding into new markets.

They mentioned you're both tackling [market] right now and facing similar challenges around [specific issue].

[Name] implemented our process on their last 5 deals and caught 3 potential margin killers before getting quotes.

Here's what they said: "That one analysis step just saved me $28K on this deal. Worth every penny."

Worth exploring for your expansion?

[Your Name]

P.S. — [Name] is expanding to 3 new markets in 2025. Probably worth staying connected there too.
```

**Why this works**:
- Name-drops mutual connection (credible)
- Specific result ($28K save)
- Direct quote (more believable)
- Implies peer is already using it (social proof)

**Why this works**:

- P.S. opens door to next conversation

---

### EMAIL 6: Direct Ask (Day 10, 9 AM)

**Subject**: Let's cut to the chase

**Body**:

```
Hi [FirstName],

I think we could help you avoid $12K-$18K in margin loss on your [market] expansion based on the deals you've done historically.

I'd like to show you three things in 15 minutes:

1. The exact analysis process that caught 3 margin killers for [mutual contact]
2. How other successful investors in [region] are approaching [market]
3. What post-expansion optimization typically looks like (results-wise)

No pitch. Just showing you what's possible.

How's Thursday at 2 PM or Friday at 10 AM for a quick call?

[Your Name]
[Your Phone]

P.S. — If you're slammed with the expansion prep, I totally get it. Just let me know what day works better.
```

**Why this works**:
- Specific value proposition
- Clear what you'll show (not vague)
- Two time options (easy yes)
- Validates their busy schedule (reduces friction)

---

### EMAIL 7: Breakup (Day 14, 4 PM)

**Subject**: Should I close your file?

**Body**:

```
Hi [FirstName],

I'm going to assume [market] expansion planning isn't a priority right now, and that's totally fine.

I'll close your file on my end unless I hear otherwise.

For what it's worth, we typically see the best results when investors nail down their analysis process BEFORE entering a new market (not after 3 deals in and losing money).

So if you decide to revisit this, might be worth a quick conversation now.

But no pressure either way. Best of luck with the expansion.

[Your Name]

P.S. — If there's someone else on your team I should be talking to about this instead, happy to reconnect with them.
```

**Why this works**:
- Honest and respectful (not needy)
- Creates slight FOMO (timing matters)
- Offers alternative (PS) if he's not the right person
- Closes loop without aggression
