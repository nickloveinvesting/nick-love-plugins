## Carousel-Specific Hook Types

These six hook types are optimized for carousel formats where the hook must drive swiping behavior, not just attention.

### Hook Type 1: The Question Hook

**Psychology:** Questions activate the brain's completion instinct. Relevant questions create an open loop that demands resolution via swiping.

**Formula:** [Provocative question that challenges assumption] + [Visual that stops scroll]

**Examples:**
- "Your business does $1M in revenue. Why are you still the bottleneck?"
- "What do business owners with 20+ hours of free time per week know that you don't?"
- "You've tried 3 coaching programs. Why did none of them work?"

**Best for:** Problem-aware audiences, coaching/consulting, debate-driving content

### Hook Type 2: The Shock / Statistic Hook

**Psychology:** Unexpected numbers create cognitive dissonance. The brain needs resolution.

**Formula:** [Surprising number] + [Implication that affects them directly]

**Examples:**
- "83% of business owners earning $1M+ work more hours than their employees. Here's why."
- "Slide 3 is the moment everything changed."
- "$1.8B in funded deals. Zero came from hustle culture."

**Best for:** Data-driven content, credibility building, proof-based carousels

### Hook Type 3: The Promise Hook

**Psychology:** Explicit value exchange. Removes friction from the decision to swipe.

**Formula:** [Specific outcome] + [Mechanism or timeframe] + [Proof element]

**Examples:**
- "The 5 systems that replaced me as the bottleneck in my business (steal all 5)."
- "How to add $500K in revenue without adding a single employee."
- "Save this: the exact operating system behind 7,000+ business engagements across 30 years."

**Best for:** Framework delivery, listicles, save-optimized content

### Hook Type 4: The Step-by-Step / List Hook

**Psychology:** Numbered formats signal structured, digestible value. Lower perceived cognitive cost.

**Formula:** [Number] + [Specific outcome] + [Swipe indicator]

**Examples:**
- "5 decisions that separate $500K businesses from $2M businesses."
- "The 3-step framework that replaces hiring with structure."
- "From revenue plateau to scale: 7 slides that show the exact shift."

**Best for:** Educational content, tutorials, how-to carousels

### Hook Type 5: The Mistake / Myth Hook

**Psychology:** Fear of doing something wrong is a stronger motivator than desire for improvement.

**Formula:** [Common action they are doing] + [Why it is wrong / costing them]

**Examples:**
- "You're adding people to a broken system. Here's what happens."
- "The hiring mistake that costs $500K+ business owners $200K per year."
- "Stop building your business around your calendar. Here's what to build it around instead."

**Best for:** Contrarian positioning, myth-busting, high-comment carousels

### Hook Type 6: The Curiosity / Cliffhanger Hook

**Psychology:** Open loops. The brain cannot rest until the loop is closed.

**Formula:** [Incomplete story or teased revelation] + [Promise of payoff on a specific slide]

**Examples:**
- "I lost $3M in 2008. What I did next with $600 built an empire. Slide 4 is the turning point."
- "This one framework replaced 3 coaching programs, 2 masterminds, and a $25K consultant."
- "We stopped doing this one thing. Revenue doubled in 90 days."

**Best for:** Story arcs, personal brand content, transformation narratives

## General Viral Hook Patterns

19 proven patterns applicable across all content types. Adapt these for carousel format by keeping the hook under 10 words and ensuring it creates a swipe-worthy open loop.

### 1. Proxy Learning (Study/Collect)

**Psychology:** "I did the research so you don't have to."

**Templates:**
- "I study [topic] so you can copy them"
- "I collect [topic] like [unexpected comparison]"
- "I've analyzed [number] [items] so you don't have to"

**Best for:** Educational content, frameworks, curated lists

### 2. Popular Name

**Psychology:** Authority by association. Well-known names create instant credibility and curiosity.

**Templates:**
- "[Famous person] uses this [thing] and has over [percentage] [outcome]. Today I'm sharing it for free"
- "[Famous brand] used this strategy to secretly [action] their users"
- "I cracked the [industry] code [prestigious group] are gatekeeping"

**Best for:** Authority borrowing, curiosity hooks

### 3. Authority Credibility

**Psychology:** Social proof + expertise = trust. Numbers validate authority.

**Templates:**
- "I run a [number]-person [business]. Here are [number] things I would never do again"
- "After [timeframe] in [industry], here's what nobody tells you"
- "Built [achievement] with [constraint]. Here's the blueprint"

**Best for:** LinkedIn, thought leadership, experience-based advice

### 4. Tools/Resources List

**Psychology:** Practical value. People love tools that save time.

**Templates:**
- "Here are [number] tools that will save you [time/money]"
- "[Number] [resources] every [role] should bookmark"

**Best for:** Quick-win content, listicles, resource roundups

### 5. Cautionary Tale

**Psychology:** People learn from others' mistakes. Stories create emotional connection.

**Templates:**
- "I [action] and [negative outcome]. Here's what I learned"
- "My friend [did action] in [timeframe]. After [timeframe], [consequence]"

**Best for:** Personal stories, vulnerability marketing, relatability

### 6. Analysis-Based Insights

**Psychology:** Research = credibility. Data satisfies our need for proof.

**Templates:**
- "I analyzed [number] [items]. Here are the top [number] [insights]"
- "After studying [number] [examples], [surprising finding]"

**Best for:** Data-driven content, research findings, industry insights

### 7. Achievement with Constraint

**Psychology:** Constraints make achievements more impressive.

**Templates:**
- "How I [achievement] in [timeframe] while [constraint]"
- "[Result] with [limitation]. Here's how"

**Best for:** Aspirational content, case studies, growth stories

### 8. Steal My Process

**Psychology:** People want proven systems they can copy. "Steal" implies exclusivity.

**Templates:**
- "Steal my [process] to [outcome]"
- "The exact [system] I use to [result]"

**Best for:** Tactical content, processes, frameworks

### 9. Proxy Learning (Action-Based)

**Psychology:** Vicarious learning. "I took the risk so you can learn safely."

**Templates:**
- "I [did action] so you don't have to. Here's what I learned"
- "Spent [resource] on [thing] so you can skip it"

**Best for:** Product reviews, comparisons, recommendations

### 10. Myth-Busting

**Psychology:** Contrarian views grab attention. People love being "in the know."

**Templates:**
- "The biggest lie you've been told about [topic] is to [action]"
- "[Common belief] is completely wrong. Here's why"

**Best for:** Contrarian takes, thought leadership

### 11. Reset Expectations

**Psychology:** "Clean slate" thinking. Permission to start fresh.

**Templates:**
- "Forget everything you know about [topic]"
- "Ignore all the advice about [topic]. Do this instead"

**Best for:** Disruptive content, new frameworks, paradigm shifts

### 12. Opposite/Contrarian

**Psychology:** Cognitive dissonance creates curiosity.

**Templates:**
- "[Authority]'s [public advice] contradicts their [internal practice]"
- "Everyone tells you to [action]. I did [opposite] and [result]"

**Best for:** Debate-starters, personal brand building, viral potential

### 13. Data-Driven Insights

**Psychology:** Numbers feel objective and trustworthy. Surprising data creates curiosity.

**Templates:**
- "After studying [number] [items], we found [surprising stat]"
- "[Percentage] of [group] don't know this about [topic]"

**Best for:** Research-backed content, surprising stats

### 14. The Unexpected

**Psychology:** Pattern interruption. Finding uncommon threads creates insight.

**Templates:**
- "The one thing I noticed about every [successful group]"
- "Every [high-performer] does this [unexpected thing]"

**Best for:** Pattern recognition, insights, thought leadership

### 15. You're Losing

**Psychology:** Loss aversion is powerful. Fear of missing out drives action.

**Templates:**
- "You're losing [metric] by [common action]"
- "Every [timeframe] you don't [action], you lose [result]"

**Best for:** Problem-aware audiences, urgency, wake-up calls

### 16. Tiny Change, Big Impact

**Psychology:** Small actions feel achievable. Disproportionate results create curiosity.

**Templates:**
- "One [small action] can [big result]"
- "A [tiny change] can [massive outcome]"

**Best for:** Quick wins, optimization content, tactical tips

### 17. Everything Wrong

**Psychology:** Challenge assumptions. Comprehensive problem identification creates trust.

**Templates:**
- "Everything you know about [topic] is wrong, because [surprising truth]"
- "Your entire approach to [topic] is backwards. Here's why"

**Best for:** Paradigm-shifting content, bold claims

### 18. Less Beats More

**Psychology:** Simplification is appealing. "Doing less" feels counterintuitive.

**Templates:**
- "[Group] with less [thing] outperform those with more [thing]"
- "I cut [thing] in half and [surprising positive result]"

**Best for:** Counterintuitive insights, efficiency content

### 19. Behind-the-Scenes Testing

**Psychology:** Specific personal sacrifice details (late nights, long hours) create authenticity. The reader thinks "they actually did the work."

**Templates:**
- "I stayed up until [time] [timeframe], [testing/comparing action]"
- "I spent [duration] [timeframe], [running specific experiment]"

**Best for:** Product comparisons, tool reviews, experiment results, AI/tech content

## Direct-Response Hook Templates

Three fill-in-the-blank templates engineered to hijack System 1 thinking, create severe curiosity gaps, and agitate pain points. These are designed for high-conversion carousels specifically.

### Template A: The Contrarian Myth-Buster (Anchoring + Authority)

Creates an immediate pattern break by challenging a deeply held belief. Generates cognitive dissonance that forces swiping to see the justification.

**Structure:** "Everything you thought about [topic] is wrong. Here is why [Common Practice] is actually destroying your [desired outcome]."

**Example:** "Everything you thought about short-form video is wrong. Here is why posting 3x a day is actually destroying your algorithm ranking."

**Best for:** Niche-specific content, positioning as contrarian authority

### Template B: The Direct Consequence Agitator (PAS Focus)

Leverages loss aversion — people are more motivated to avoid a loss than acquire a gain. Highlights a secret consequence of a common mistake.

**Structure:** "Stop doing [action] right now. It is secretly causing [Undesirable Consequence]. Instead, use this [Number]-step framework to [desired outcome]."

**Example:** "Stop using generic hashtag walls right now. It's secretly causing the algorithm to flag your account as spam. Instead, use this 3-step SEO framework to guarantee For You Page delivery."

**Best for:** Problem-aware audiences, urgency-driven content

### Template C: The Insider Solution (Curiosity Gap + Ultra-Specificity)

Positions the creator as someone who did the exhausting labor of trial and error. Ultra-specific numbers build immediate credibility.

**Structure:** "Tired of [pain point]? We tested [Large Number] versions of [thing] so you don't have to. Here is the only one that actually [desired outcome] in [timeframe]."

**Example:** "Tired of your carousel hooks falling flat? We tested 47 different hook structures so you don't have to. Here's the only format that consistently breaks 60% STR."

**Best for:** Product comparisons, tool reviews, framework delivery

## Carousel-Specific Hook Rules

These rules override general hook writing rules when creating carousel content:

1. **Maximum 10 words on slide 1** (ideal: 8). General social posts allow up to 120 characters. Carousels do not. The hook must be readable in under 0.7 seconds.
2. **The hook must create a swipe-worthy open loop.** Unlike video hooks that keep viewers watching, carousel hooks must make swiping feel inevitable.
3. **Pair hook pattern with slide 2 bridge.** The hook creates the question; slide 2 must immediately validate it and pull forward. Never let slide 2 repeat the hook.
4. **Use specific numbers in hooks.** "5 decisions" beats "several decisions." "83%" beats "most." Specificity = believability at scroll speed.
5. **Write 10 hook versions before selecting.** Volume beats filtering. The best hook is rarely the first one written.
6. **A/B test hooks.** Same carousel content, different hook, posted one week apart. The winner typically gets 2-4x higher reach.
