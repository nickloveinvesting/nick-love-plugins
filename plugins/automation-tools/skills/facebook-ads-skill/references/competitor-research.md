# Competitor Research & Ad Analysis

## Overview

This guide covers how to systematically research, extract, and analyze competitor ads to inform your own campaigns. The goal is inspiration and insightтАФnever direct copying.

---

## Facebook Ad Library Research

### Accessing the Ad Library

**URL:** https://www.facebook.com/ads/library

**Filters Available:**
- **Country:** Filter by geographic targeting
- **Platform:** Facebook, Instagram, Audience Network, Messenger
- **Media Type:** All, Images, Videos, Memes
- **Active Status:** Active ads only, or all ads
- **Date Range:** When ads started running

### Search Strategies

**1. Direct Brand Search**
```
Search: "Tony Robbins"
Result: All ads from Tony Robbins' pages
```

**2. Keyword Search**
```
Search: "real estate investing"
Result: All ads containing this phrase
Filter: By advertiser to find competitors
```

**3. Industry Scan**
```
Search multiple competitors:
- [Competitor 1]
- [Competitor 2]
- [Competitor 3]
- [Industry publication]
- [Industry influencer]

Compare: Themes, formats, messaging approaches
```

### What Long-Running Ads Tell You

Ads that have been active for 30+ days are likely profitable. Pay special attention to:

- **90+ days active:** This is working extremely well
- **60-90 days active:** Solid performer, proven concept
- **30-60 days active:** Working, worth studying
- **<30 days active:** Too early to judge, could be testing

**How to Check:** Look at "Started running on [date]" in Ad Library

## Systematic Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Extract & Screenshot

For each competitor, capture:
- All active ads (screenshot or screen record)
- Ad copy (primary text, headline, description)
- Visual style (colors, imagery type, format)
- CTA button used
- Landing page URL

### Step 2: Categorize by Theme

Group ads into themes to identify patterns:

**Common B2B/Coaching Themes:**
- Pain point / Problem awareness
- Transformation / Results
- Social proof / Testimonials
- Educational / How-to
- Urgency / Scarcity
- Authority / Credentials
- Free resource / Lead magnet
- Direct offer / Sales

**Example Categorization:**
```
Competitor: Grant Cardone
Total Active Ads: 47

Themes:
- Wealth/Income transformation: 18 ads (38%)
- Free training/webinar: 12 ads (26%)
- Social proof/lifestyle: 9 ads (19%)
- Book/product promotion: 5 ads (11%)
- Event promotion: 3 ads (6%)
```

### Step 3: Analyze Messaging Patterns

For each competitor, document:

**Pain Points They Target:**
```
1. "Stuck at the same income level" - appears in 8 ads
2. "Trading time for money" - appears in 6 ads
3. "No clear path to wealth" - appears in 5 ads
4. "Fear of economic uncertainty" - appears in 4 ads
```

**Value Propositions Used:**
```
1. "Financial freedom in X months"
2. "Proven system used by X students"
3. "Works even if you have no experience"
4. "Without [common objection]"
```

**Social Proof Patterns:**
```
- Student testimonials (video): 12 ads
- Income/results screenshots: 8 ads
- Celebrity/expert endorsements: 4 ads
- Company logos/media mentions: 3 ads
- Follower/subscriber counts: 2 ads
```

### Step 4: Creative Pattern Analysis

**Visual Patterns to Track:**

| Pattern | Description | Frequency |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| UGC Style | Raw, phone-recorded feel | High |
| Professional Studio | Polished, branded look | Medium |
| Text Overlay | Bold text on image/video | High |
| Before/After | Split screen transformation | Medium |
| Screen Recording | Software/results demo | Low |
| Lifestyle | Success imagery | Medium |

**Video Structure Patterns:**
```
Pattern A: Hook тЖТ Pain тЖТ Solution тЖТ Proof тЖТ CTA
Pattern B: Hook тЖТ Story тЖТ Transformation тЖТ CTA
Pattern C: Hook тЖТ Proof тЖТ Proof тЖТ Proof тЖТ CTA
Pattern D: Hook тЖТ Curiosity тЖТ Value тЖТ CTA
```

**Copy Length Patterns:**
```
Short (0-50 words): Quick hit, single benefit
Medium (50-150 words): Story + benefit + CTA
Long (150-300 words): Full narrative, multiple proof points
Very Long (300+ words): Detailed sales letter style
```

### Finding Your Positioning Gap

After analyzing competitors, identify:

1. **What everyone says:** The commodity messages to avoid
2. **What no one says:** Potential differentiation opportunities
3. **What only we can say:** Unique proof points, story, credentials

## Ethical Guidelines

### DO:
тЬЕ Use Ad Library for research and inspiration
тЬЕ Identify patterns and principles that work
тЬЕ Adapt successful concepts to your unique voice
тЬЕ Document insights for your team
тЬЕ Track what's working in your industry
тЬЕ Find gaps in competitor messaging

### DON'T:
тЭМ Copy ad copy verbatim
тЭМ Steal creative assets (images, videos)
тЭМ Clone landing pages exactly
тЭМ Claim competitor testimonials as your own
тЭМ Use competitor trademarks inappropriately
тЭМ Plagiarize unique frameworks or proprietary content

### The Adaptation Rule

When you see something that works, ask:
1. **What principle makes this work?** (Not what specific words)
2. **How does this apply to our audience?** (Not their audience)
3. **What's our unique version?** (Not their version)

## Quick Reference: Research Workflow

**Weekly (15 min):**
- Quick scan of top 3 competitors
- Note any new ads or changes
- Screenshot anything interesting

**Monthly (1 hour):**
- Full Ad Library review of all competitors
- Update competitor analysis documents
- Identify patterns from long-running ads
- Add to creative inspiration queue

**Quarterly (2-3 hours):**
- Comprehensive trend analysis
- Multi-competitor comparison update
- Identify positioning gaps
- Plan tests based on insights
